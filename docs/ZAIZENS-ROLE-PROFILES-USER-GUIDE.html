Introduction

  3. Usernames are not case-sensitive — lariza and Lariza both work.
  4. After login you are taken to your role portal; use the sidebar or app launcher to open modules.
  5. Always sign out on shared computers when finished.

Cashier — Staff Guide

Collect payments, issue prepay tickets, print receipts, manage wallets and payment codes.

LoginLariza
Password12345
Main screen/cashier

Daily workflow

  1. Open the HMS login page and sign in with your Cashier credentials.
  2. You land on the Cashier portal — click Cashier Command Center or go to /cashier.
  3. Use the Pending tab to find patients awaiting payment; search by name or ticket number.
  4. Click Collect or Issue payment to open the prepay modal — add services from the catalog.
  5. Select a payment method (Cash, MoMo, Wallet, BetterPay, etc.) and confirm collection.
  6. Print the receipt and share the payment code with Lab, Radiology, or Pharmacy as needed.
  7. Use Cash Top-up (after Petty cash payout) to credit patient wallets at the desk.
  8. End of day: open EOD reconciliation to review totals before sign-out.

Tips & reminders

  • Wallet payments require the patient to have an active wallet with sufficient balance.
  • BetterPay: wait for patient confirmation on their phone before marking payment received.
  • Payment codes link OPD orders to Lab/Radiology/Pharmacy — always verify code status before redirecting patients.

Doctor — Staff Guide

Consult patients, record clinical notes, order lab/radiology tests, prescribe medication, and admit IPD.

LoginAwelsa
Password12345
Main screen/opd-queue

Daily workflow

  1. Sign in — you are redirected to the Doctor portal (/portal/doctor).
  2. Open OPD Queue to see patients waiting or in consultation.
  3. Select a patient and click Start consultation — record vitals review, history, and examination.
  4. Add orders (lab tests, imaging, procedures) from the consultation workspace.
  5. Write prescriptions — send to Pharmacy after saving the consultation.
  6. Mark consultation Complete — patient may need Cashier payment before lab/radiology.
  7. For admissions: create an IPD admit order from the patient chart or IPD module.
  8. Use Ward rounds and IPD orders for in-patients under your care.

Tips & reminders

  • Patients must pay (or have insurance coverage) before lab/radiology can process orders.
  • Use the clinical chart (<code>/patient/:id</code>) for full history, allergies, and documents.
  • Teleconsultation links are available when enabled by your administrator.

Nurse — Staff Guide

Triage OPD patients, record vitals, support ward care, and assist IPD medication workflows.

LoginGhong
Password12345
Main screen/opd-queue

Daily workflow

  1. Sign in — open the Nurse portal (/portal/nurse).
  2. Go to OPD Queue — identify patients in Registered or Waiting status.
  3. Perform triage: update priority, chief complaint, and nursing notes.
  4. Record vital signs (BP, pulse, temperature, SpO₂, weight) on the patient chart.
  5. Move patient to Waiting for doctor when vitals and triage are complete.
  6. For IPD: open Ward board — assist admissions, bed assignment support, and nursing care plans.
  7. Administer and document IPD medications per doctor orders.
  8. Flag abnormal vitals or emergencies using ER shortcuts when urgent escalation is needed.

Tips & reminders

  • Always verify patient identity (name, ID band, date of birth) before procedures.
  • Vitals saved on the chart are visible to the consulting doctor immediately.
  • Use the HMS Hub for a quick overview of today's OPD and IPD workload.

Radiologist — Staff Guide

Process imaging worklist, verify payment codes, upload reports, and finalise radiology studies.

LoginTef
Password12345
Main screen/radiology

Daily workflow

  1. Sign in — open the Radiology portal (/portal/radiology).
  2. Go to Radiology worklist (/radiology) for pending imaging requests.
  3. Verify the patient's payment code or paid status before performing the study.
  4. Open the request — confirm clinical indication and prior imaging if relevant.
  5. After acquisition, attach images or link from PACS as configured.
  6. Enter the radiology report (findings, impression, recommendations).
  7. Mark the study Final — the ordering doctor and patient chart are updated.
  8. Use filters (date, modality, status) to manage daily workload efficiently.

Tips & reminders

  • Unpaid orders appear with payment pending — direct patient to Cashier first.
  • Critical findings should be communicated to the referring clinician per hospital protocol.
  • Reports are visible on the patient chart once finalised.

Lab Technician — Staff Guide

Receive lab requests, verify payments, enter results in LIMS, and release final reports.

LoginSedrick
Password12345
Main screen/lab

Daily workflow

  1. Sign in — open the Lab portal (/portal/lab).
  2. Open LIMS / Lab worklist (/lab) for pending test requests.
  3. Confirm payment code or cashier receipt before sample collection/processing.
  4. Register or scan sample ID linked to the patient request.
  5. Enter results per test parameter — use reference ranges shown on screen.
  6. Validate abnormal flags and repeat critical values per SOP before release.
  7. Mark tests Complete — results flow to the patient chart and ordering doctor.
  8. Print lab report copies for patients when requested at the lab desk.

Tips & reminders

  • OPD lab orders from doctors require payment unless covered by insurance/waiver.
  • Use barcode or request ID search for fast lookup during busy periods.
  • Pending vs completed filters help prioritize STAT and emergency requests.

Pharmacist — Staff Guide

Dispense prescriptions, verify payments, manage stock alerts, and counsel patients on medications.

LoginBerinyuy
Password12345
Main screen/pharmacy

Daily workflow

  1. Sign in — open the Pharmacy portal (/portal/pharmacy).
  2. Go to Pharmacy dispensing (/pharmacy) for the active prescription queue.
  3. Locate the prescription by patient name, Rx number, or payment code.
  4. Verify payment or insurance approval before dispensing.
  5. Check stock availability — substitute per formulary policy if items are out of stock.
  6. Record quantities dispensed and batch numbers where required.
  7. Mark prescription Dispensed — inventory is decremented automatically.
  8. Provide patient counseling on dosage, timing, and side effects at the counter.

Tips & reminders

  • Doctor prescriptions from OPD consultations appear after consultation is saved.
  • Low-stock alerts are visible on the pharmacy dashboard — reorder via procurement if permitted.
  • Partial dispensing is supported when full quantity is not immediately available.
