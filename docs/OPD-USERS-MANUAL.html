1. OPD Overview & Roles

The Outpatient Department (OPD) manages same-day patient visits. Standard flow: patient pays consultation fee → Front Desk adds visit to queue → Nurse records vitals → Doctor consults → patient pays for orders → Lab/Radiology/Pharmacy process → Doctor completes visit.

🖥
Front Desk Register patients, add OPD visits, assign rooms, route to cashier
💳
Cashier Collect consultation fee and order payments; issue receipts & payment codes
🩺
Nurse / Nursing Aid Triage, record vitals (BP, temp, weight, SpO₂, pulse)
👨‍⚕️
Doctor Consultation, diagnosis, orders, prescriptions, complete visit
🔬
Lab / Radiology Verify payment codes, enter results, finalise reports
💊
Pharmacy Dispense paid prescriptions; OPD medication alerts

Patient Arrives → Front Desk Check-in → [Registration if new] → Cashier (Consult Fee) → Front Desk (Add to OPD Queue) → Nurse (Triage/Vitals) → Doctor (Consultation) → [Orders?] → Cashier (Pay Orders) → Lab / Radiology → Doctor (Review) → Pharmacy → Completed

Step-by-step (all roles)

  1. Cashier — Collect consultation fee. Issue receipt to patient. cashier.write
  2. Front Desk — Register patient. Add to OPD Queue under correct department. opd.write
  3. Nurse — Select patient. Record vitals: BP, Temp, Weight, SpO₂, Pulse. Status → Waiting Doctor. nursing.write
  4. Doctor — Open consultation. Record notes, diagnosis, orders (lab/imaging/prescriptions). consult.write
  5. Cashier — Patient pays for all investigation and medication orders before processing.
  6. Lab / Radiology — Run tests; upload results to patient chart.
  7. Pharmacist — Dispense paid prescriptions.
  8. Doctor — Review results. Finalize diagnosis. Mark visit Completed.
Footnote: Station 2 — Existing patients skip to Station 4 (Cashier). New patients go to Station 3 (Registration) first. After payment → Station 5 (OPD Queue).

Carry-forward rule

Patients not consulted yesterday are automatically returned to today's active queue each morning with a renewed queue number (first in line). Use Return to today's queue from the visit menu when needed.

4. Front Desk Procedures

Opening the OPD queue

  1. Sign in and open Clinical → OPD queue or /opd-queue.
  2. Review Active Queue — Today for current visits.
  3. Use + New Visit to add a patient after consultation fee is paid.

Adding a new visit

  1. Confirm patient paid consultation fee at cashier (unless emergency waiver).
  2. Click + New Visit on OPD queue.
  3. Search and select the patient.
  4. Choose department and referring doctor if applicable.
  5. Save — patient receives a queue number and status Registered.

Existing vs new patients

  • Existing patients — Front Desk check-in only; skip registration.
  • New patients — Register via /patients first (demographics, contact, ID).

Visit registry & filters

The Visit Registry section lists historical visits. Filter by name, ticket, date range, status, or department. Sort newest/oldest as needed.

Emergency visits: Tick Emergency / Waiver at registration to bypass consultation prepayment. See Emergency module guide for full A&E workflow.

5. Cashier & Payment Codes

Consultation fee (OPD entry)

Standard OPD: patient pays consultation fee first, then returns to Front Desk for queue registration. Without valid payment, vitals and consultation may be blocked.

Payment codes

Each paid service generates an alphanumeric payment code (e.g. CON-4829-K7HM3R9Q). Codes are used to:

  • Verify payment before lab, radiology, or pharmacy
  • Track partial payments on multi-line tickets
  • Support follow-up consultations within validity rules

Collecting payment for orders

  1. Patient returns from doctor with new orders.
  2. Load payment code at /cashier or Cashier portal.
  3. Review line items (lab, radiology, pharmacy, procedures).
  4. Accept full or partial payment — balance stays on same code.
  5. Print receipt; patient proceeds to departments.

Insurance co-pay

When insurance is on the patient chart, the cashier applies configured insurer share % automatically.

6. Nursing & Triage

Starting triage

  1. On OPD queue, locate the patient card (status Registered).
  2. Click Start Triage or Triage / Vitals.
  3. If payment is invalid, system shows Payment required — send patient to cashier first.
  4. Enter vitals: blood pressure, temperature, weight, SpO₂, pulse rate.
  5. Save — status advances to Waiting Doctor.

Vitals on the card

After recording, the card shows vitals timestamp. Nurses cannot edit vitals once the patient is in consultation or waiting for doctor (locked state).

Optional vitals

Vitals may be optional for some workflows, but doctors typically require them before consultation opens. Front Desk staff without clinical write permission see queue cards in read-only mode.

Assign consultation room

Nursing or Front Desk can assign a physical room from the patient card when rooms are configured. Room appears on waiting-screen TV.

7. Doctor Consultation

Opening a consultation

  1. From OPD queue, click Consult on your assigned patient (or unassigned queue).
  2. System opens /consultation-new with patient and visit context.
  3. Record chief complaint, history, examination, diagnosis.
  4. Add orders: laboratory, radiology, prescriptions, procedures.
  5. Mark external when care is outside the hospital.
  6. Complete consultation — payment code generated for new orders if applicable.

Doctor-assigned queue split

When enabled, doctors see Assigned to you separately from other physicians and unassigned visits. Your patients' cards highlight with a green border.

Consultation prerequisites

  • Valid consultation payment (or emergency waiver)
  • Vitals recorded (for statuses triage / waiting doctor)
  • Patient not already completed or cancelled

Follow-up vs new consultation

Returning patients may qualify for Follow Up under prior payment code if documented and validity rules pass. Otherwise patient needs new consultation payment — use New Consultation (payment).

8. Lab, Radiology & Pharmacy

After doctor orders

  1. Patient pays at cashier (Station 8).
  2. Lab technician verifies payment code → enters results → finalises.
  3. Radiologist verifies code → uploads report → finalises.
  4. Pharmacist dispenses paid prescriptions.
  5. Doctor reviews results on chart and advances visit toward completion.
Unpaid orders: Lab and radiology cannot process unpaid orders. Send patient to cashier first.

OPD orders billing modal

Doctors and cashiers can open the Orders bill modal from the visit card to review line items, refunds, or consolidated billing for the visit.

9. Consultation Rooms

Physical consultation rooms link to doctors and display on OPD cards and the lobby waiting screen.

Configure rooms (Admin)

  1. Open Settings → Room configuration (/admin/consultation-rooms).
  2. Add room: code (e.g. R1), display name, department, sort order.
  3. Assign doctors who use that room.

Assign on visit

From the patient card menu: Assign consultation room. Override auto-detected room for this visit. Waiting screen shows room and queue number.

10. Follow-Up Visits

Follow-up allows a returning patient to consult without a new consultation fee when:

  • Prior visit documented follow-up eligibility
  • Payment code still valid (uses / max uses / date rules)
  • Doctor selects Follow Up from visit menu

If follow-up is blocked, the system shows a message — patient must pay for a new consultation.

11. OPD Treatment & Drug Chart

For multi-day outpatient treatment with scheduled doses:

  • Doctor: /opd/treatment/:visit_id — start treatment, prescriptions, dose schedule
  • Nurse: /opd/chart/:visit_id — administer doses, drug chart
  • Terminate active treatment before starting a new course on the same visit

Medication administered triggers alerts to the prescribing doctor when configured.

12. Completing a Visit

  1. All orders paid and processed (or explicitly cancelled/documented).
  2. Doctor reviews results and final diagnosis.
  3. From visit menu: Mark Completed.
  4. Status → Completed; patient leaves OPD flow.

Use Cancel Visit only when visit should not proceed — requires confirmation.

Record death links to death registry for in-hospital mortality documentation when applicable.
