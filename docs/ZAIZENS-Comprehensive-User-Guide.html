Introduction

Welcome to the ZAIZENS Integrated HMS Comprehensive User Guide. This document explains how to perform everyday tasks in the hospital system — from registering a patient to closing an inpatient stay. Your administrator controls which menus and actions you see based on your role and permissions.

Before you begin: Sign in with your staff username and password. Use Sign out on shared computers. If a menu item is missing, contact your system administrator — do not share credentials with colleagues.

1. Getting Started

Signing in

  1. Open your hospital HMS URL in a modern web browser.
  2. Enter your username and password.
  3. After login you are redirected to your role's home portal (e.g. Front Desk, Doctor, Cashier).

Language

The system supports English and French. Switch language from the profile or language switcher where available. Many screens, flash messages, and this guide's in-app counterparts are fully translated.

Facility context

If your hospital operates multiple sites, your session is tied to one facility. Patient lists, stock, and charges are scoped to the active facility unless your administrator configures otherwise.

Your profile

Access /profile or /my-profile from the user menu (top right) to update personal settings and view account information.

4. Patients & Clinical Chart

Register a new patient

  1. Go to Patients (/patients) or use Register Patient on your portal.
  2. Complete demographics: name, date of birth, gender, contact, address.
  3. Save — the system assigns a patient ID.

Search existing patients

Use the search bar on the patient directory. Search by name, ID, or phone number. Click a row to open the patient profile.

Clinical chart

From the patient record, open the Clinical Chart to view and add:

  • Allergies and medical history
  • Vitals timeline
  • Consultation notes
  • Lab and radiology results
  • Prescriptions
  • Insurance policies and co-pay percentages
  • Uploaded external documents (outside lab/imaging reports)

Patient directory actions

Depending on permissions, you may: view chart, manage insurance, open credit accounts, enable patient portal access, edit profile, or delete records (admin only).

Insurance

Set the primary insurer and insurer share % on the chart or via /insurance. Co-pay is applied automatically at the cashier.

5. OPD Queue & Visits

The OPD (Outpatient Department) queue at /opd-queue manages same-day patient visits.

Adding a visit

  1. Ensure the patient has paid the consultation fee at the cashier (unless emergency waiver applies).
  2. From OPD queue, add the patient to today's queue under the correct department.
  3. The visit receives a queue number and status Registered.

Visit statuses

Registered → Triage → Waiting Doctor → In Consultation → Orders Pending → Billing → Completed

Triage (Nurse / Front Desk)

  1. Select the patient card on the OPD queue.
  2. Record vitals: BP, temperature, weight, SpO₂, pulse.
  3. Status advances to Waiting Doctor.

Consultation

The doctor opens the visit, starts or continues a consultation, records notes and orders, then marks the visit appropriately.

Completing a visit

After all orders are paid, processed, and reviewed, the doctor marks the visit Completed.

Carry-forward

Patients not consulted yesterday may be automatically returned to today's queue with a renewed queue number (first in line).

Assign consultation room

Front desk or nursing can assign a physical consultation room on the patient card. Rooms are configured under Settings → Room configuration.

6. Consultation Rooms

Consultation rooms link physical spaces (e.g. Room 1, Room 2) to one or more doctors. Assigned rooms appear on OPD patient cards and the waiting-screen TV.

Configure rooms (Admin / Nurse / Director)

  1. Open Settings → Room configuration (/admin/consultation-rooms).
  2. Click Add room: enter code (e.g. R1), display name, optional department, sort order.
  3. Check all doctors who use that room (multiple doctors per room supported).
  4. Save — repeat for each physical consultation space.

Assign room on OPD queue

On the patient card, use Assign consultation room to override the auto-detected room for that visit.

If no rooms appear on OPD cards, verify that at least one room exists in Room configuration and is marked active.

7. Cashier & Payment Codes

Cashier workspace

Open /cashier or the Cashier portal. The workspace loads patient charges and accepts payment.

Payment codes

Each paid service generates an alphanumeric payment code (e.g. CON-4829-K7HM3R9Q). Codes are used to:

  • Verify payment before lab, radiology, or pharmacy processing
  • Track partial payments on multi-line tickets
  • Support follow-up consultations within validity rules

Collecting payment

  1. Load or scan the payment code at the cashier.
  2. Review line items (consultation, lab, radiology, pharmacy, etc.).
  3. Accept full or partial payment — remaining balance stays on the same code.
  4. Print receipt for the patient.

Consultation fee (OPD entry)

Standard OPD flow: patient pays consultation fee first, then returns to front desk for queue registration.

Insurance co-pay

When a patient has insurance on file, the cashier applies the configured insurer share automatically.

Patient wallets

Pre-paid wallet balances at /wallet can be deducted at point of service where enabled.

8. Nursing & Vitals

Recording vitals

  1. From OPD queue or /nursing/vitals, select the patient.
  2. Enter: blood pressure, temperature, weight, SpO₂, pulse rate.
  3. Save — vitals appear on the patient card and clinical chart.

IPD nursing

On the ward board and IPD medication module:

  • Receive newly admitted patients and record baseline vitals
  • Execute care plans and administer medications
  • Respond to IPD nurse message inbox (/ipd/inbox)
  • Process clinical department alerts for pending orders

Nurse roster

View your duty schedule at /nurse-roster (permission required).

9. Doctor Consultations

Start a consultation

  1. From OPD queue or /consultation-new, select the patient visit.
  2. Record chief complaint, history, examination findings, and diagnosis.
  3. Add orders: laboratory tests, radiology imaging, prescriptions, procedures.
  4. Mark external care when services are performed outside the hospital.
  5. Complete the consultation — a payment code may be generated for new orders.

Follow-up consultations

Returning patients may qualify for follow-up under a prior payment code if the doctor documented follow-up eligibility and payment validity rules are met. Use the follow-up OPD path when eligible; otherwise the patient needs a new consultation payment.

Prescriptions

Issue prescriptions from the consultation or standalone registry at /prescriptions. Prescriptions require an active consultation for OPD patients unless follow-up or IPD/ER rules apply.

Admissions

From consultation or patient chart, issue an admission request with admitting diagnosis to begin the IPD workflow.

Ward rounds

On the IPD board, review inpatients daily, update notes, and place new orders on the running credit tab.

Discharge

Issue Clinical Discharge with a discharge summary when the patient is medically fit. Financial discharge by cashier must follow before the bed is released.

10. Laboratory & Radiology

Laboratory workflow

  1. Open Laboratory portal (/portal/lab) or LIMS hub (/lims).
  2. Verify the patient's payment code for the ordered test.
  3. Enter structured results using test templates where available.
  4. Finalise the result — this notifies the referring clinician and may update the patient portal.

Radiology workflow

  1. Open Radiology portal (/portal/radiology).
  2. Verify payment code from the imaging order.
  3. Upload report and images; use reporting templates.
  4. Finalise and release to the patient chart.
Important: Lab and radiology cannot process unpaid orders. Send the patient to the cashier first if payment is pending.

IPD and emergency alerts

Inpatient and emergency orders generate alerts accessible from sidebar links under Lab/Radiology when you have write permission.

11. Pharmacy

Dispensing workflow

  1. Open Pharmacy portal (/portal/pharmacy).
  2. Locate the prescription — verify payment if required.
  3. Dispense medicines and record quantities in the system.
  4. Link to inventory when stock module is enabled.

Prescription verification

Pharmacy staff can verify prescription QR codes at /hms/prescription-verify or /verify/rx.

IPD/ER medication alerts

Respond to ward and emergency medication alerts from the pharmacy sidebar when configured.

12. Inpatient (IPD) Care

Hospitalization module at /ipd manages the full inpatient lifecycle.

Admission sequence

  1. Doctor — admission request with diagnosis
  2. Cashier — collect admission deposit; credit tab opens
  3. Front Desk / ADT — assign ward and bed on the ward board (/wards)
  4. Nurse — receive patient, baseline vitals, care plan

During stay

  • Daily doctor ward rounds and new orders
  • Lab, radiology, pharmacy charges auto-added to running bill
  • IPD medication chart at /ipd/medication
  • IPD census at /ipd/census

Discharge sequence

  1. Doctor — Clinical Discharge + summary
  2. Cashier — final bill settlement, Financial Discharge, bed freed
  3. Front Desk / ADT — confirm departure, bed → Available
Both clinical and financial discharge are mandatory before the patient physically leaves.

13. Emergency / A&E

Rapid registration

  1. Register the patient quickly on OPD queue or emergency module.
  2. Tick Emergency / Waiver — this bypasses prepayment requirement.
  3. System auto-routes to Emergency/A&E with status Waiting Doctor.

Concurrent care

Nurse records vitals immediately (BP, Temp, SpO₂, Pulse, GCS) while the doctor begins assessment and treatment. Orders are placed on a credit tab.

Disposition

Once stabilised, the doctor either:

  • Admits to IPD (follow IPD workflow), or
  • Discharges with documented disposition

Billing

Cashier presents retrospective bill to family and settles all emergency charges.

The Emergency/Waiver checkbox must be selected at registration to bypass consultation prepayment.

14. Appointments & Scheduling

Book an appointment

  1. Open /appointments from the sidebar or Front Desk portal.
  2. Search for the patient and select date, time, department, and doctor.
  3. Confirm booking — patient receives queue placement on visit day.

Slot configuration

Administrators configure available slots at /hms/appointments/slots-config.

Patient online booking

Patients can self-book via the public portal at /portal/login when enabled.

Waiting screen

Lobby TV display at /hms/waiting-screen shows queue numbers and consultation rooms.
