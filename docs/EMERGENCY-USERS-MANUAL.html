Introduction

Welcome to the ZAIZENS Integrated HMS Emergency User Manual. This document covers the full Accident & Emergency (A&E) workflow — designed for critical patients where prepayment is bypassed and care begins immediately on a credit tab.

Critical rule: For emergency patients, select Emergency / Waiver at registration (or use A&E Quick Registration). This activates prepayment bypass and routes the case to the Emergency board — not standard OPD cashier-first flow.

1. Emergency Overview & Roles

Emergency / A&E manages critically ill or injured patients with concurrent nursing and medical assessment. Orders and medications accumulate on an open credit tab until disposition and cashier settlement.

🩺
Nurse / Nursing Aid Immediate vitals, acuity assignment, bed assignment, continuous monitoring
👨‍⚕️

🔬
Lab / Radiology Urgent investigations — STAT priority; results to chart immediately
💊
Pharmacy Stat doses, emergency medications on credit tab
💳

2. Four-Phase ER Workflow

The revamped Emergency module follows four clinical phases on each visit chart (/emergency/visit/:id):

Phase 1 — Arrival · Quick registration · Triage

Patient arrives → quick reg (existing or new/unknown) → acuity level, vitals, flags, chief complaint, bed assignment.

Phase 2 — Parallel orders · SOAP consultation

Doctor assessment concurrent with nursing. Place STAT lab, radiology, pharmacy, blood bank, or procedure orders on credit tab.

Phase 3 — Disposition

Once stabilized: discharge home, short stay (SSU), admit IPD, direct to OT, transfer out, deceased, or left without being seen (LWBS).

Phase 4 — Charge capture · ER closure · Bill settlement

Real-time charges on open tab → confirm disposition → cashier retrospective settlement → visit closed.

Arrival → Triage (Acuity + Vitals) → Doctor SOAP + Orders (concurrent) → Disposition → Charges settled at Cashier → [Admit to IPD if required]

3. Eight-Step Emergency Process

Role-based steps aligned with workflow guides and live ER board:

  1. Front Desk — Rapid registration. Tick Emergency / Waiver. No prepayment — billing exception created. opd.write
  2. System — Auto-routed to Emergency/A&E. Status → Waiting Doctor. ER widgets update on dashboards.
  3. Nurse — Record vitals immediately: BP, Temp, SpO₂, Pulse, GCS, pain score. Concurrent with doctor. nursing.write
  4. Doctor — Immediate assessment and treatment. Rapid orders (IV, bloods, imaging, meds) on credit tab. consult.write
  5. Lab / Radiology — Process urgent investigations. Upload results immediately to chart.
  6. Pharmacist — Dispense emergency medications. Costs on credit tab.
  7. Doctor — Once stabilized: admit to IPD, or discharge with documented disposition.
  8. Cashier — Present retrospective bill. Settle all emergency charges. Issue receipt.

6. Quick Registration

A&E Quick Registration modal

  1. Click New emergency patient on Emergency board.
  2. Phase 1 — Arrival: choose existing patient or new/unknown.
  3. For new: enter name, gender, phone (or Unknown), chief complaint.
  4. Assign doctor (or any available).
  5. Submit Register & start triage — bypasses cashier prepayment.

From OPD queue

When adding an OPD visit, tick Emergency — system prompts switch to A&E Quick Registration with waiver message.

Unknown / walk-in

Use New / Unknown path for unidentified patients; complete demographics when available during care.

7. Phase 1 — Triage

On the ER visit chart, complete Phase 1 · Triage:

  1. Set Acuity Level (L1–L5).
  2. Enter vitals: BP sys/dia, pulse, SpO₂, temp, respiratory rate, GCS, pain 0–10.
  3. Set triage flags (trauma, cardiac, stroke, etc.).
  4. Record Chief complaint (clinical).
  5. Assign bed in resuscitation, holding, observation, or SSU bay.
  6. Save triage — vitals sync to nursing chart for OPD consultation rules.

Update triage as condition changes. Critical results trigger alerts on the board.

8. Phase 2 — SOAP & Parallel Orders

Consultation workflow

  1. Mark Doctor Seen — starts door-to-doctor KPI timer.
  2. Create consultation — SOAP note required before prescriptions and some clinical docs.
  3. Concurrent nursing continues vitals and monitoring.
  4. Use Phase 2 · Parallel Orders for urgent requests.

When clinically stable, choose a disposition pathway:

Enter clinical summary and confirm disposition. Visit timeline records arrived, doctor first seen, and door→doctor minutes.

10. Phase 4 — Charges & Settlement

Phase 4 · Charges on the ER visit chart:

  • View all line items on open credit tab
  • Add manual charges (catalog, pharmacy search, miscellaneous)
  • Mark items settled as cashier processes payment
  • Total open tab displayed until closure

After disposition, visit moves toward clinical discharge state — chart becomes read-only for clinical edits; awaiting financial settlement.

EMG settlement tickets

Cashier workspace shows emergency patients with unpaid charges. Filter A&E badge cases for pending settlement.

11. Medico-Legal Cases (MLC)

For assault, RTA, poisoning, burns, sexual assault, and other notifiable cases:

  1. Flag visit as MLC on ER chart.
  2. Complete MLC form: case type, incident time/place, brought by, police details.
  3. Document examination, injuries, provisional diagnosis.
  4. Notify police when required.
  5. Sign & Lock — record becomes read-only; print triplicate copies.
Once MLC is locked, it cannot be edited — only viewed and reprinted. Open MLC count appears on Emergency board KPIs.

12. ER → IPD Admission

When disposition is Admit to IPD:

  1. Doctor confirms admitting department and diagnosis.
  2. System creates IPD admission request.
  3. ER charges may transfer to IPD running bill when configured.
  4. ADT assigns ward and bed on ward board.
  5. Follow IPD User Manual for deposit, nursing, and discharge workflows.

Maternity emergencies may link to labor ward and neonatal pathways with pay-later deposit rules.

13. Retrospective Billing

After clinical care

  1. Patient or family directed to cashier when medically appropriate.
  2. Cashier filters Emergency / A&E pending tickets.
  3. Review full credit tab: consultations, orders, medications, procedures.
  4. Collect payment or apply insurance co-pay.
  5. Issue receipt — mark EMG settlement complete.
Emergency flow intentionally defers payment until after stabilization — unlike standard OPD consultation-first payment.

14. KPI Dashboard

ER KPI dashboard at /emergency/kpi tracks:

  • Door-to-doctor time (median / SLA breaches)
  • Left without being seen (LWBS) rate
  • ER → IPD conversion rate
  • Critical alert counts
  • MLC cases open
  • Credit tab totals across active visits

Use for shift handover, quality improvement, and hospital management reporting.

15. Doctor ER Alerts

Doctors receive real-time alerts at /doctor/er-inbox for:

  • Stat order completions
  • Medication administration notifications
  • Assigned ER patients requiring review

Red ER alerts strip appears on clinical pages when pending items exist.
